PhD Position

ARCADES Network ESR (Early-Stage Researcher)

Title: Interactive geometric design under constraints imposed by function and fabrication
Adviser: Prof. Helmut Pottmann
School: Technische Universität Wien, Austria
Brief description: Form-finding in geometric design is a process informed by various constraints, from which an appropriate polyhedral mesh is obtained. These constraints can come from a range of impositions, e.g. geometric properties, aesthetic wants, or practical necessities by way of manufacture or function. Depending on how these impositions are modelled, the overall constraint variety takes on some degree in each of the variables of the model.
This ARCADES project has two main tasks, based on previous research on form-finding for polyhedral meshes: the first is to develop insight in simplifications of a constraint variety, and the second is to use those insights to investigate representations of "non-smooth" surfaces.

Masters Thesis

Title: From Green's Functions to Chord Spaces, for the \(2\)-Disk
Adviser: Prof. C. F. Bödigheimer
School: Universität Bonn, Germany
Brief description: Let \(\mathbf{D}^{2}\) be the \(2\)-disk, as it sits in the standard \(\mathbf{R}^{2} \cong \mathbf{C}\), and let \(\mathcal{Z} = \{\zeta_{j}\}_{j=1}^{n} \subset \mathbf{D}^{2}\). On \(\mathbf{D}^{2} \smallsetminus \{\zeta_{j}\}_{j}\), there is a harmonic function with singularities at \(\mathcal{Z}\): \[g_{\mathcal{Z}} \left(z\right) := \sum_{j=1}^{n} \ln \left|\frac{\zeta_{j} - z}{1 - \overline{\zeta_{j}} z}\right| .\] It can be shown that this function has, with multiplicities, \(n-1\) critical points in \(\mathbf{D}^{2} \smallsetminus \{\zeta_{j}\}_{j=1}^{n}\). Looking at the flow lines of the gradient of \(g_{\mathcal{Z}}\) around a critical point \(\rho\), there are certain flow lines that approach \(\rho\) from \(\partial \mathbf{D}^{2}\), the boundary of \(\mathbf{D}^{2}\), and certain flow lines that recede from \(\rho\) toward some singularity or another critical point. The main task was to understand the relationship between those singular points \(\mathcal{Z}\) and the points of \(\partial\mathbf{D}^{2}\) which flow approaching some critical point.
