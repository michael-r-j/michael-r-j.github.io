Where goes the tangible in memory? Can the physical be a consolation or a remediation?
An analog image comes to be through sensitivity and vulnerability in ways that the digital inherently tries to circumvent: the sensitive film, the tactful development, the vulnerable image. In this way analog photography is more poignant and, as a process, much more capable of reflecting the human experience. The physicality of the analog process retains a quality of representation that escape digital expression.
