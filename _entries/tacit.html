The boundaries to the digital have become ever more blurred.  In those novel spaces it has created for us, the digital has also begun to reach into the definitions of our selves and into imaginations of our identities: as we consume it, the digital consumes us.  However, it holds us in ways that we can never hold it back.
A CCTV camera is set up in the hallway, connected to a display behind a physical privacy screen.  What you see is not what is, but it is what was.  The installation is an invitation to be held for a little while: to watch, to be watched, to be stored, to be displayed, and to be released into digital ether.

Curated by Julia Harrauer, who also designed the exhibition poster.
