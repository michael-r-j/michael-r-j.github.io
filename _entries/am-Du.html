A hand is pushed up through layers of abstraction, from an analog 35mm film photo to a rasterized digitalized image, and then pulled back down again into the tangible by exposure onto a photopolymer and etching into a copper plate for intaglio printing.
We find our selves at our boundaries, at the imperfect/vulnerable interface to others, muddled by memory of the lost and piqued by presence of the found. In this way, we become through flows between the concrete and abstract, from interactions both shaping and being shaped. These translations and transfigurations aim towards comfort or stability of self, but are innately full of conversion and, ultimately, loss.
This work is a material meditation on this phenomenon, particularly pushing and pulling through the abstract veils of the digital, to consider what it can mean to represent and interact with transmutations of image.

As my hand reaches out to touch yours, when can only a figment reach back? And, when does this inconsistency stop being human?
